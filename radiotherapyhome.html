Thank you for trying out the Cancer Research UK augmented reality activity! Every day our researchers work hard to solve difficult problems and save lives. This is your chance to step into the shoes of a researcher and try to figure out a problem of your own.

Cancer Research UK was a key player in the development of radiotherapy. See if you can solve this puzzle and figure out how many patients in the UK benefit every year from radiotherapy. To find the answer, you'll need to follow a couple of steps

First, you'll need to physically arrange those four squares so that when you look at them through this device, they form the 3D image of a radiotherapy mask, sort of like a 3D puzzle. The fifth square shows you what the mask should look like when it's complete.

Once you've done that, click the "I think I've got it!" button to see whether you can figure out the answer to a question about our life-saving research.

Cancer Research UK was a key player in radiotherapy, which now benefits more than ____________ patients every year in the UK.

Hint - Try looking through the mouth of the original example mask from different angles. Do you see those groups of black squares? Do any of those patterns match the one you made with your own, larger squares?

How To Play

Try pointing your phone at some of those puzzle squares you printed and cut out. Pretty cool huh? Four of these squares can be arranged to make a 3D model of a plastic mask worn during radiotherapy. Think of it like a puzzle - see if you can put the mask pieces together the right way. The square with the numbers on it is important too. It shows you the different patterns your other squares might make once you've completed the mask. Compare the shape you've made with these shapes to figure out the answer to this question!
